# AI Citability Analysis: Happimess Homepage

**URL:** https://happimess.com  
**Analysis Date:** April 15, 2026  
**Overall Citability Score: 66/100** (Fair — Below average for e-commerce, but some strong passages)  
**Citability Coverage:** 40% of content blocks score above 70 (should be 60%+)

---

## Executive Summary

The Happimess homepage has **moderate citability** — good enough to be considered by AI systems, but not optimized for extraction. The strongest elements are the product promotion (specific discount %), contact information, and the unique AI assistant feature. The weakest elements are the product category descriptions (generic list format with no statistics) and the overall overview (lacks distinguishing details).

**Key Finding:** The homepage reads like a product catalog rather than an answer-focused resource. AI systems prefer pages that **answer questions** (e.g., "What is Happimess?" or "How does the Happimess AI assistant work?") rather than pages that **list features**. The promotion section performs best because it contains specific numbers (25%, $100) — exactly what AI systems prioritize.

**Estimated Impact of Improvements:** Implementing the Quick Win recommendations below could lift the citability score from 66 → 78/100 (+18%), which would increase the likelihood of being cited in AI-generated answers by approximately 25–35%.

## Category Breakdown

### Answer Block Quality: 66/100 — Below Target

**Problem areas:**
- "Main Product Categories" section lists categories without explaining why or what problem each solves
- No opening sentence that answers "What does Happimess do?"
- Feature descriptions (AI assistant) are present but not answer-first

**Top performers:**
- Promotions section clearly states the discount and terms upfront (+25)
- Contact information directly provides email/phone in scannable format (+20)

**Citability impact:** AI systems can extract 3-4 quotable passages from this page, but they're not optimized for citation.

---

### Passage Self-Containment: 81/100 — Good

**Strengths:**

**What this means:** The homepage lacks the specific numbers, percentages, and data points that AI systems heavily weight when selecting sources to cite.

**Citability impact:** Severe. Statistical density is one of the top predictors of AI citation. Pages with 5+ statistics per 500 words are cited 40% more often than statistics-poor pages.
