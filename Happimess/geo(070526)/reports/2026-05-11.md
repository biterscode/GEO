### llms.txt Detail (Major Win)
The deployed `llms.txt` is well-structured and goes beyond the basic standard — it includes Universal Commerce Protocol (UCP) agent instructions, an MCP endpoint (`/api/ucp/mcp`), and a `/.well-known/ucp` discovery file. This gives AI agents structured programmatic access to the store, which is ahead of most e-commerce competitors.

### New Observation
- **No JSON-LD schema on homepage or About page** — confirmed absent in this snapshot. The entire site appears to lack sitewide Organization schema in `theme.liquid`.
- **Blog posts lack Article/BlogPosting schema** — the `why-choosing-the-right-trash-bag` article has ~2,100 words and good H2 structure but zero structured data. This is a missed opportunity for rich results.
- **Author attribution** — still "From The Mess Experts" (a team label, not a named author). Google and AI crawlers prefer named, credentialed authors for E-E-A-T signals.
- **H1 on homepage** — not definitively detected; Shopify theme may render it in a hero banner as styled text rather than a semantic `<h1>`. Worth verifying in Shopify code.

## Top 3 Actions for Next Sprint

1. **Fix Organization schema in `theme.liquid`** — Add `sameAs` array with all 6 social profile URLs (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, Pinterest, YouTube, TikTok). Also fix `description: null`. This is a 30-minute Shopify code edit with All-platform impact.

2. **Add BlogPosting schema to blog article template** — Wire up `datePublished`, `author` (use brand name "Happimess" or create an Author schema for "The Mess Experts"), `headline`, and `wordCount`. Dates are now visible in HTML — just need the schema.

3. **Unblock `/policies/` in robots.txt** — Remove the `Disallow: /policies/` line. Policy pages (privacy, terms, refund) are trust signals that AI systems use for brand authority scoring.
