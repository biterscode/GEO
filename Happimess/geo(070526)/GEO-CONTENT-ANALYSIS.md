## Priority Recommendations

### Recommendation 1 — CRITICAL: Fix the FAQ 404 and author page 404

Two broken pages, two lines of effort:
1. Create `/pages/faq` or redirect it to an existing FAQ section — this page is linked in the footer of every page on the site
2. Create `/pages/asodariya-sumi` with a real author bio — this URL is embedded in the Article schema of every blog post and returns 404

These are not content improvements — they're broken links. Fix them before anything else.

### Recommendation 2 — HIGH: Add FTC disclosure to all commercial blog posts

Every blog post that recommends a Happimess product needs a disclosure statement. This is both an FTC compliance requirement and a Google spam policy concern. Add to the top of affected posts:

> *"Disclosure: This article was written by the Happimess team. Some products mentioned are sold by Happimess. We recommend products we believe will be valuable to our readers."*

### Recommendation 3 — HIGH: Name one author and build their credibility

Replace "From The Mess Experts" with one named individual across all 26 blog posts. Create a live author page at `/pages/[author-name]` with: full name, photo, 3–4 sentence bio (background in home organization, interior styling, or product design), and a LinkedIn link. Add Article schema author fields. This single change compounds across all 26 posts — one hour of setup, site-wide E-E-A-T improvement.

### Recommendation 4 — HIGH: Rebuild the buying guide to 2,000+ words

The kitchen trash can buying guide at 650 words is the flagship commercial content piece — highest commercial intent, most competitive query. It needs:
- Remove the off-topic wipes product H3s
- Expand to 2,000–2,500 words
- Add a documented evaluation methodology ("how we select products")
- Add a comparison table (step-open vs sensor vs push-lid × plastic vs stainless × price tier)
- Add external citations (EPA guidelines, NKBA kitchen design standards)
- Add specific model recommendations with reasoning
- Add a "what we ruled out" section (signals critical thinking)

### Recommendation 5 — MEDIUM: Remove emojis from H2 headings in the dual-can guide

Replace all 10 emoji-bearing H2 headings with clean text headings. This improves AI passage extraction, professional credibility, and heading consistency across the blog.

### Recommendation 6 — STRATEGIC: Publish one piece of original research

Commission a 100-response customer survey on kitchen trash habits (what size can do you have, how often do you empty it, what problems do you have). Publish as: "We Surveyed [N] Households About Kitchen Trash — Here's What We Found." Include: methodology section, charts, cross-tabulations by household size. This single post provides original data that every AI citation system can cite with attribution, a backlink magnet for home organization publications, and a foundation for updating all other posts with internal citations.
